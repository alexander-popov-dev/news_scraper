31 December, 2025

2025 Qualification Examinations for Patent Attorneys of Georgia – Statistical Overview

By Order No. 327 dated 29 September 2025, the Chairperson of the National Intellectual Property Center of Georgia (“Sakpatenti”) appointed the first qualification examinations for patent attorneys of Georgia in the general and trademark specializations.

During the registration period for the examination – from 1 October to 14 November 2025, inclusive – a total of 47 applications were submitted: for the trademark specialization – 42, and for the general specialization – 5 applications. Due to non-compliance with the qualification requirements, 3 applications were rejected.

Accordingly, a total of 44 applicants successfully completed registration for both specializations. Prior to the examination, 2 applicants requested to cancel their registration for the examination, which was granted.

Mr. Soso Giorgadze Awarded the finalist team “Swish” of the Millennium Innovation Award 2025

The “Millennium Innovation Award” was held for the eleventh time this year. For many years, Sakpatenti has cooperated with the Millennium Foundation, serving as a constant and active supporter of the competition and being closely involved in the implementation of initiatives aimed at fostering innovation and the development of new technologies. Since the establishment of the Award in 2014, a representative of Sakpatenti Mr. Vasil Giorgadze, Head of the Mechanics and Physics Division of the Department of Inventions and New Varieties and Breeds, has been a permanent member of the jury. In addition, Sakpatenti has participated in the evaluation of competition projects and selection of the winning teams for the fifth consecutive year. Each year, Sakpatenti awards one of the finalist teams of the competition

16 December, 2025

Sakpatenti Participated in a Training Workshop Organized by the China National Intellectual Property Administration

Within the framework of the “Belt and Road” initiative, a Training Workshop on Intellectual Property, organized by the China National Intellectual Property Administration (CNIPA),was held in Chongqing, the People’s Republic of China. The aim of the event was to create such platform for exchanging information and experience, that would allow the participants to get acquainted with the issues related to the intellectual property policy, strategy and IP protection of China. From Georgia, Ms. Tika Nebieridze, Deputy Chairperson of the National Intellectual Property Center of Georgia – “Sakpatenti”, and Ms. Darejan Mtchedlidze, Head of the Trademarks, Geographical Indications and Design Department attended the event
